Frequently Asked Questions

What happens when I hit my character limit?

Your character allowance resets on the 1st of each month. If you reach your limit mid-month, you can upgrade your plan on Patreon for immediate access to a higher allowance, or wait for the reset.

What are Studio voices and why do they cost 5×?

Studio voices are Google's highest-quality narration voices — they sound remarkably human. Because they cost significantly more to generate, each character of Studio audio counts as 5 standard characters against your monthly allowance. For example, 2M characters on Archmage gives you 400K characters of Studio audio (or any mix of Studio and non-Studio).

Can I change my plan anytime?

Absolutely. Just update your Patreon pledge amount and your tier adjusts automatically the next time you visit the app. Upgrades take effect immediately.

What counts as a “character”?

Every character in your text counts — letters, spaces, punctuation, and line breaks. A typical novel page is around 1,500–2,000 characters. The Scribe plan at 500K characters handles roughly 250–330 pages per month.

Is the free tier really free forever?

Yes. The free tier gives you 500 characters per month with the Adam (Standard) voice, no credit card required. It’s perfect for trying out the service before committing to a plan.

What’s the difference between Chirp 3: HD and other voices?

Chirp 3: HD is Google’s latest-generation voice synthesis. These 30 voices are the most natural-sounding options available — they handle emphasis, pacing, and emotion better than any previous generation. Available starting at the Bard tier.

Do I need a Patreon account?

Only if you want a paid tier. Create a free account on Text to Storyteller, then link your Patreon from the Profile page when you’re ready to upgrade. The free tier works without Patreon.

Can I use the audio commercially?

Commercial use (podcasts, YouTube, audiobooks for sale, etc.) is available on the Archmage ($99/mo) and Deity ($249/mo) plans. Lower tiers are for personal use only.
